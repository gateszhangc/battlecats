# Gilded Mischief

Gilded Mischief treats information like a ceremonial climb: dense, playful, and exacting, with every mark arranged as if it were pressed into metal by a master engraver. The visual world is built from stacked geometry, coin-like circles, narrow beams, and quiet cat-ear silhouettes. It should feel meticulously crafted, not illustrated casually; the tower is less a mascot and more an artifact, a small monument made through countless patient refinements.

Space is dramatic and vertical. Forms rise from dark ground into concentrated gold, using stepped layers and precise alignment to imply progress, pressure, and reward. The composition must show painstaking attention to proportion: no element floats accidentally, every gap feels measured, every decorative mark supports the climb. Text is sparse and functions as stamped notation rather than explanation.

Color carries the emotional system. Deep roasted black, lacquer brown, hot amber, and polished gold create the sense of a rare limited event without copying any official game imagery. The palette should be calibrated like metal under changing light: bright enough to signal treasure, restrained enough to remain elegant. The final assets must look like the product of deep expertise, with master-level execution in gradients, edges, and contrast.

Rhythm comes from repetition: floors, coins, rays, small reference ticks, and compact labels. These repeated parts create the feeling of a map for a climb, rewarding close looking without becoming cluttered. Craftsmanship is the core requirement. The mark should appear labored over with care, as if every line was redrawn until it became inevitable.

The cat reference remains subtle. A pair of ears, a rounded face, or two small eyes may appear, but never as borrowed character art and never as a cartoon copy. The identity should be original, abstract, and legally safe while still instantly suggesting a mischievous tower challenge. The finished work should be museum-clean, web-practical, and unmistakably made for a gold tower.
